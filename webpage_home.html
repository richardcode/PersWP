RICHARD MILLAR

Richard Millar

Climate System Scientist

I am a climate system scientist working on how insights from the climate science can be successfully embedded in effective climate and energy policy.

My research spans the physical and economic consequences of climate policy and aims to investigate robust pathways to achieving global climate goals of net-zero emissions.

About

I am currently a James Martin Fellow at the University of Oxford. I am part of the Oxford Martin Net Zero Carbon Investment Initiative, which aims to connect the latest insights from climate science and economics to transition metrics for companies towards net zero carbon emissions.

I completed my DPhil in the Climate Dynamics group of the Department of Physics at the University of Oxford, where I worked on investigating uncertainty in the climate response to carbon dioxide and the implications for mitigation policy.
